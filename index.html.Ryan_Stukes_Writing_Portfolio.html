Appalachian State University Student Brings Africana Music to Downtown Boone in Groundbreaking Cultural Event

Rhythm & Roots: A Celebration of Music, Culture & Community draws 50+ attendees, earns 5-star ratings, and spotlights the untold Black history of the Appalachian region

BOONE, N.C., April 4, 2025 — Ryan Stukes, a graduating senior at Appalachian State University, successfully produced and executed Rhythm & Roots: A Celebration of Music, Culture & Community, a live cultural event held at The Happy Persimmon in Downtown Boone as part of the University Honors College's senior thesis program...

"This was an amazing event!!! The atmosphere was so positive and welcoming. I loved seeing a bit into your thesis project — I can tell this is such a passion of yours and that was truly inspiring."

— Rhythm & Roots Attendee Survey Respondent

Bringing Underrepresented Voices to the High Country

Stukes' honors thesis examined the historical lack of visibility and representation of Africana music — encompassing jazz, funk, neo-soul, Afro-Latin, and West African traditions — in the predominantly Appalachian music landscape of Western North Carolina.

A Multi-Channel Campaign Executed on a Zero-Dollar Budget

Stukes developed and executed a comprehensive multi-channel campaign including digital flyer distribution across 19 King Street businesses, campus-wide digital signage reaching 140+ screens, and coordinated Instagram co-publishing with event partners.

Community Stakeholder Update

Demonstrates: Empathetic stakeholder communications, resource organization, audience sensitivity, and clear calls to action.

Context: Written during the Communications Manager Intern role at Watauga Arts Council in response to Hurricane Helene. Published to the online directory and distributed via email newsletter.

Supporting High Country Artists: Hurricane Helene Relief Efforts

In the aftermath of Hurricane Helene, the devastation has deeply impacted our NC High Country community. Our thoughts are with everyone in Western North Carolina as we slowly begin to rebuild. Among those most affected are our local artists, whose contributions are often overlooked in times of crisis.

The Hope After Helene Arts Relief Fund

Watauga Arts Council has released its own initiative in response to Hurricane Helene. Many artists and nonprofit organizations have lost equipment and income. This fund aims to provide critical financial support helping artists recover and continue their work in rebuilding the community.
